# Human Energy Model

## Introduction

GrowthDigitalTwin models human performance using an energy-based approach.

Energy represents the available capacity required for:

- Thinking
- Learning
- Physical activity
- Emotional regulation
- Personal development

---

# Energy State

The system defines:

E(t)

as the available energy at time t.

The energy state changes based on:

- Recovery
- Activities
- Stress
- Environment

---

# Energy Sources

Energy increases through:

- Quality sleep
- Nutrition
- Exercise recovery
- Spiritual practices
- Positive relationships

---

# Energy Consumption

Energy is consumed by:

- Physical activity
- Mental work
- Stress
- Decision making
- Social demands

---

# Energy Balance

Future mathematical model:

Energy at time t+1 depends on:

Current energy
+
Energy gained
-
Energy consumed
-
Entropy losses

---

# Energy Optimization

The system aims to answer:

- Where is energy being lost?
- Where should energy be invested?
- How can future goals be achieved?
